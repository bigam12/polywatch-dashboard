📖 Wallet Label Glossary

NO Fader
This wallet bets NO on more than 60% of its trades. It profits when the market overestimates how likely something is to happen — e.g. a market says YES is 80% likely, they bet NO and win when it doesn't happen.
Trigger: ≥60% of recent buys are NO side
YES Buyer
This wallet predominantly bets YES (65%+ of trades). Typically backs the favored or expected outcome. Reliable when following on high-confidence markets.
Trigger: ≤35% of recent buys are NO side
Late Entry
This wallet buys when the market is nearly settled — average entry price above 68¢. Lower risk, lower reward. They're confirming what's already likely, not discovering it early.
Trigger: avg entry price ≥68¢
Early Bird
This wallet enters markets when outcomes are still uncertain — average entry price below 38¢. Higher risk, higher reward. They often spot value before the crowd does.
Trigger: avg entry price ≤38¢
Contrarian
This wallet bets against heavy market favorites. They buy NO at prices below 30¢ — meaning the market has YES priced above 70¢ — and win when the consensus is wrong. High risk, high signal value when they're right.
Trigger: ≥35% of buys are NO at <30¢ (YES >70¢)
Selective
This wallet trades infrequently (8–35 total positions) but maintains a high win rate (62%+). They pick their spots carefully. A signal from this wallet carries more weight — they don't bet unless they're confident.
Trigger: 8–35 total trades AND win rate ≥62%
High Volume
This wallet has made 150+ resolved trades. Very active. Their win rate is based on a large sample, making it statistically more reliable — but their edge per trade may be thinner.
Trigger: ≥150 closed positions
Grade System (S / A / B / C / D)
S — Win rate >65% AND ROI >20%  |  A — Win rate >55% AND ROI >10%
B — Win rate >45%  |  C — Win rate >35%  |  D — Below C thresholds
